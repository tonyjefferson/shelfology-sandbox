Additional Floating Shelf Installation FAQs

What if I have a small shelf that I want to place where there are no studs?

Although we don't recommend it, it may happen that the sweet spot you've been dreaming of placing your floating shelf just happens to be between two studs.

In this case, we would simply suggest that you install your shelf using ALL of the included wall anchors.

Can I hang a floating shelf with Command Strips?

Bless those geniuses at 3M, but no, you absolutely should NOT install floating shelves with command strips.

Kitchen

Kitchen

The kitchen is where floating shelves live or die based on how well you plan them.

Depth: 8–12 inches handles most kitchen needs. A 10–12 inch deep shelf is the sweet spot for standard dishware. If you're storing pots or large jars, go deeper — our Aksel HD bracket handles shelves up to 14 inches deep.

Height: First shelf should sit 18–24 inches above your countertop. If you're stacking two or three shelves, keep 12–15 inches between each one.

What to put on them: Daily-use items only. Plates, glasses, mugs, spice jars, a few cookbooks. If it comes out once a week or less, it belongs in a cabinet.

Watch out for: Grease and steam from the stove. Keep kitchen shelves a bit away from the cooktop if you can, and give them a quick wipe when you're cleaning up anyway.

Bathroom

Bathroom

Bathrooms are one of the best places for floating shelves because every bathroom is short on wall space and long on need for it.

Depth: 6–8 inches is plenty. You're storing toiletries, small towels, candles — nothing deep. A lighter-duty bracket like our Aksel LD is a great fit here.

Height: Keep them at least 24 inches above the toilet if they're in that zone. Above a sink, stay 12+ inches above the faucet or mirror so nothing gets in the way.

Material matters: Bathrooms are humid. If you're going with wood, make sure it's sealed or finished properly. Unfinished wood in a bathroom is a recipe for warping. All of our finished hardwood options hold up well in bathroom conditions.

Living Room

Living Room

Living room shelves are mostly about display, which means aesthetics matter more here than in the kitchen or bathroom.

Depth: 6–10 inches works well. You don't need deep shelves for books and decor — and shallower shelves keep the room feeling open.

Height: If they're above your couch, go 10–12 inches above the back of the couch. For a general display wall, keep the center of your arrangement around eye level — about 4 to 5 feet off the floor.

Styling: Leave some empty space. A shelf that's 70% full looks intentional. A shelf that's 100% full looks cluttered. Group things in threes, vary the heights of your objects, and let the shelf breathe.

Home Office

Home Office

A floating shelf above a desk or on a nearby wall keeps reference books, a plant, and a few personal items within reach without taking up any desk space.

Depth: 6–8 inches is the sweet spot. Enough for books standing up, not so deep that it sticks out and crowds the room.

Height: Above a desk, 12–18 inches above the desk surface. On a side wall, eye level while seated — about 4 feet from the floor.
